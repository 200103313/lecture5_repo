Traditions of Kazakh nationality

wedding traditions

A wedding is an important stage in
the life of any person, but for the
Kazakhs it is an even more important event,
which is accompanied by a huge number of
customs and rituals. Long before the wedding,
the parents of the future newlyweds conspire.
This is followed by matchmaking and the
payment of "kalym" for the bride.
The wedding itself is also divided into two:
a wedding at the bride's house and
a wedding at the groom's house.

Family traditions

Family for Kazakhs is the basis of life.
Family relations, the upbringing of children,
the principle of family ties - all this is reflected
in the special customs and traditions of the Kazakh people.
The principle of "zheti ata", according to which one
should know seven generations of one's ancestors,
as well as indicating the continuation of the family
line through the paternal line. There were special
rules in the upbringing of children, and significant
events in the life of a child were accompanied
by peculiar Kazakh rituals.

Household and sports traditions

The everyday life of the Kazakhs was filled
with many minor but very interesting traditions,
which formed a unique ethnic culture.
The people had customs and traditions
that were used at home, on the street, at important events.
There were also various games that also
became folk traditions - children's
street games, horse games, etc.

Age-old traditions and customs of the Kazakh people

The Kazakhs are a nomadic people who
today have switched to a settled way of life,
but carefully preserve their traditions that
have been formed over thousands of years.
Constant travel across the endless steppes,
life in small groups, peculiarities of
religious beliefs - all this created a unique
set of rituals and customs, which today are
commonly called the traditions of Kazakhstan.
Some traditions helped develop practical survival
skills, others had a sacred meaning, some were
created to develop relationships and socialization,
and some became the basis for the formation of the
culture of the Kazakh people and their identity.
