Interesting about the nature of Kazakhstan

The nature of Kazakhstan is amazing and diverse: here you can see beautiful flower steppes and majestic snow-capped mountains.
With a population of 17 million people, most of the territory remains uninhabited. Vast steppes, deserts and mountains are habitats for many species of animals and birds.

Relief and climate of Kazakhstan
The territory of the Republic is huge: from the Ural Mountains to Altai and Tarbagatai. The country is washed by the Aral and Caspian seas.
More than half of the territory is desert and semi-desert. The rest is occupied by steppes, forests and mountains. In the center is the so-called "yellow steppe" or Sary-Arka,
in the north - the West Siberian Plain, in the west - the Subdural Plateau. The lowest point is the Karagie depression, and the highest point is Mount Khan-Tengri.
Lakes and rivers are scattered among the mountain ranges and steppes: Zaisan, Zhasybay, Charyn, Ural, Ishim, etc.
The climate in the country is continental: warm summers and cold winters. There is little precipitation here: the areas are divided into arid and semi-arid.
Flora and fauna of Kazakhstan
The flora is represented by widespread and endangered species.
Often found plants:
astragalus;
sedge;
goose onion;
Ostrolodochnik and others.
Rare species of flora include wild plants: apple trees, onions, grapes, tulips.
In the wild and in reserves throughout the country, you can see wolves, foxes, elks, corsacs, argali, manul, snow leopards, Eurasian lynx, etc.
As of 2016, 125 animal species and 404 species of plants, fungi, lichens were included in the Red Book of Kazakhstan and algae.
The news often contains information about the state of the nature of the Republic, as well as about what measures are being taken to save it.
