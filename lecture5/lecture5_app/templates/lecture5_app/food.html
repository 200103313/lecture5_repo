Beshbarmak

Meat and flour dish of the Turkic-speaking peoples.
Beshbarmak is prepared, as a rule, on the occasion of family
holidays or the reception of dear guests.

Kazy

Horse meat sausage among a number of Turkic peoples, considered a delicacy,
from which chuchuk and beshbarmak are prepared. Also, the word kazy can
refer to fat from the ribs used in the preparation of this sausage.

Shuzhyk

Traditional sausages among the Turkic, Caucasian, Balkan and Middle Eastern peoples.

Sourpa

Refueling soup or meat broth among the Tajiks and Turkic peoples,
which has become widespread in the Muslim East and among the
peoples neighboring the Turks and Tajiks.

Kuyrdak

In modern cuisine, vegetables are also often added to give the dish
more volume - usually potatoes and sometimes carrots or pumpkins.
Sometimes poultry and kazy are added. First, the meat is fried, and
when half cooked, potatoes are added and stewed.

Manty

Consisting of finely chopped meat in a thinly rolled dough,
steamed in a pressure cooker

Lagman

The main feature of the lagman is hand-drawn rounded noodles
with a diameter of about 3 mm.

Bayursak

As a rule, it is prepared from unleavened or yeast dough
in the form of small donuts (diamond-shaped or round),
made by deep-frying in a cauldron.

Hospitality is the main character trait of the Kazakh people.
Even if you “run in” to a Kazakh house for a minute and on a very important matter,
the owner will still seat you at the table and offer you fragrant tea with sweets.
Respectful attitude towards guests and, most importantly, the desire to receive them in your home,
contributed to the fact that over time, the traditional Kazakh feast took the form of a ritual
that is not inferior in its philosophical and allegorical content to the world-famous Chinese tea ceremony.
